# HCA Healthcare – Equity Research Pitch
**Dodge & Cox Investment Competition | December 2024**

A long equity pitch for HCA Healthcare (NYSE: HCA) developed as part of the Dodge & Cox investment competition. The pitch argues for a buy recommendation with a price target of **$543.74**, implying **~101% upside** from the price at time of analysis.

Built by a team of 3, my contributions included industry research, supporting data analysis, and chart construction.

---

## Investment Thesis

Three core arguments support the long position:

**1. Expanding Sunbelt markets drive durable revenue growth**
HCA operates 188 hospitals concentrated in Texas and Florida, states experiencing population CAGR above 1.2% and where over 50% of America's 65+ cohort now lives. Procedure volume growth in these markets is estimated at 5–8% annually, and outpatient services (currently ~35% of revenue) are projected to drive ~12% EPS CAGR over the next three years.

**2. Operational moat is underpriced by the street**
HCA's EBITDA margin of ~19% significantly exceeds the industry average of ~14%. This margin advantage funds investments in disaster resilience, cybersecurity, and talent acquisition that competitors cannot match at scale. A conservative 0.25% annual margin improvement from 2025–2029 translates to ~$1.5B in cumulative new EBITDA contribution. The street has priced in risks from cyber breaches and natural disasters without crediting HCA's demonstrated ability to respond.

**3. Payer and geographic diversification hedges regulatory risk**
Revenue is distributed across private insurance (49%), Medicare (23%), Medicaid (16%), and uninsured (8%). This balance, combined with state-by-state geographic diversification, insulates HCA against policy shocks such as ACA expiration or site-neutral payment changes.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigant |
|------|----------|
| Regulatory changes to reimbursement rates | Diversified payer mix, long-term managed care agreements |
| Labor cost pressures (>47% of revenue) | Competitive compensation, reduced turnover by 46%, institutional partnerships |
| Operational risks (cyber, natural disasters) | Doubled cybersecurity spend, disaster response fund for employees |

## Valuation

- **Methodology:** DCF and comparable company analysis (EV/EBITDA multiples)
- **Price Target:** $543.74
- **Implied Upside:** ~101%
- **Key Driver:** Conservative outpatient volume growth + margin expansion assumptions
